Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

The genius who united logic and mathematics, laying the groundwork for modern informatics.

Here's a time line of Gottfried Leibniz life:

Early Life and Education

  • 1646: Born in Leipzig, Germany.
  • 1661: Enrolls at the University of Leipzig at age 15.
  • 1663: Earns his bachelor's degree in philosophy.
  • 1666: Publishes De Arte Combinatoria; earns his doctorate in law.

Career and Achievements

  • 1672: Studies mathematics in Paris, mentored by Christiaan Huygens.
  • 1676: Begins work in Hanover; independently develops calculus.
  • 1684: Publishes the first paper on differential calculus.
  • 1700: Helps found the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1710: Publishes Theodicy.
  • 1714: Writes Monadology, outlining his metaphysical ideas.

Death and Legacy

  • 1716: Dies in Hanover, largely unrecognized. Today, he is celebrated as a pioneer of calculus, logic, and informatics.
